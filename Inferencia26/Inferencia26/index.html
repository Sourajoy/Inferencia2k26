About us

ABOUT INFERENCIA

As an academic year neared its conclusion, a group of budding statisticians, students about to graduate from college, decided to pioneer a fest centred solely around statistics. Inferencia has provided a remarkable opportunity to delve deeper into the vast realm of statistics. The phenomenal enthusiasm of all teachers and students in this fest has never failed to amaze us.

DEPARTMENT & FACULTY

Welcome to the Department of Statistics, RKMRC Narendrapur. Taught as a pass subject as early as 1960, the Dept. of Statistics emerged as an Honours Department a couple of years later. Sri Bikash Chandra Sanyal, Sri Gourikanta Bhattacharya, Sri Sukharanjan Chakraborty, Sri Prasanta Kumar Giri and Sri Nanda Kishore De laid the foundation of the Department in 1962. Through their unwavering commitment to excellence and tireless spirit of service, the department has been able to reach where it is today. A rigorous coursework combining the better parts of traditional learning with modern innovations, ensures growth of critical thinking abilities and efficient problem-solving strategies. Teachers who prioritize the transmission of knowledge and the betterment of students not only in academics, but also in all other fields of life, all within an institution famed for fostering discipline and strong bonds of friendship, truly creates an ideal environment for holistic education.

Honorary Faculty

Dr. Prasanta Kumar Giri

The Pioneer

Professor Giri didn't just teach statistics—he built its foundation, brick by brick, book by book. His textbooks are the rite of passage for every aspiring statistician and retirement? Just an outlier in his dataset. Polite yet powerful, he turns complex theories into simple truths. In our department's grand equation, he's the undisputed constant!

Dr. Nandakishore De

The Evergreen

Professor De makes even the toughest theorems feel like child's play. No question is too silly, no doubt too trivial—he tackles them all with the patience of a saint and the sharpness of a t-test. Retirement may have claimed his title, but teaching still runs in his regression line.

Dr. Dilip Kumar Sahoo

The Umbrella

Professor Sahoo, the guardian of stats, shields his students and colleagues like a well-fitted confidence interval—always reliable, never overfitting. He teaches statistical inference, yet somehow, we fall for it like a perfect p-value. Tough? Maybe elsewhere. In his class, it's pure elegance. With wisdom as vast as his personality, he's not just a professor—he's our academic umbrella!

Faculty

Dr. Parthasarathi Chakrabarti

The Luminary

He is the King of Probability and grandmaster of randomness. When he teaches, even confusion takes notes. His lectures are intellectual adventures where students enter with doubt and exit with enlightenment. He doesn’t just explain—he illuminates, making stubborn concepts surrender to his brilliance.

Sri Tulsidas Mukhopadhyay

The Magician

Professor Mukhopadhyay steps into the classroom and suddenly, statistics isn't numbers—it's pure magic. Summing up tricky problems? Child's play. Every tough equation melts like toffee in his hands, leaving students spellbound. With a flick of his chalk, even the hardest theorems surrender—because in his class, math doesn't just add up, it dazzles!

Sri Palas Pal

The Grandmean

Professor Pal orchestrates statistics like a maestro. His lectures are front-row seats to reality via ANOVA tables. His mystical sorcery turns late-evening classes into "housefull" shows. A student’s best friend, he solves tough assignments and tougher life choices. He is the algorithm behind your success; when life throws residuals, he helps you adjust your R²!

Sri Subhadeep Banerjee

The Storyteller

Professor Banerjee believes every theorem has a tale to tell. He won't rest until every student is hooked, convinced and nodding along—resistance is futile! With anecdotes sharper than a chi-square test, he turns dry formulas into gripping sagas. For him, numbers may lie, but a good story never does!

Dr. Suryasish Chatterjee

The Torchbearer

Newest recruit Professor Chatterjee is as integral as the Central Limit Theorem. With inference in his bones and patience in his soul, he tackles doubts with a well-posed function’s elegance. Students suspect he’s asymptotic—approaching perfection, never too busy to listen. In class, logic flows smoother than a Cauchy sequence and curiosity finds a confidence interval to thrive.
